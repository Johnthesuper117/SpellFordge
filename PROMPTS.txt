SoulForge:
Guide, which explains the mechanics of the game
Start Game. In the game, the player is a wizard with a health and mana bar, starting with the spell Arcane Bolt (left-click/primary) and an Arcane Staff (right-click/secondary). The player can only have 1 secondary at a time, but up to 5 primaries. The secondaries, or Artifacts, give the player buffs, a skill activated with right click/space and requires mana to use, and can be upgraded based on  Synergy Count. Primaries, or Spells, are the player’s attacks and they use Q and E to switch between them. They will all have their own unique effects, 1-2 synergy elements, and the player can have 5 at max, so when they choose one more, they are given the choice to replace one of their older Spells with this new Spell, or not. Synergies are when you have 2 or more of the same ‘traits’ in your repertoire of Spells and Artifact, which give buffs. The user will start with the Spell Arcane Bolt, which is a blue-ish purple-ish ball that moves a bit faster than the player, deals 10 damage, and with the Arcane Synergy (which increases mana by 10 for every Synergy count starting at 2). The Artifact Arcane Staff, which passively increases mana regen by 10%, its active skill is it increases the damage of all Spells by 10%, and has the Arcane Synergy. 
Enemies: Start with some basic enemies like Goblins, (green, mid speed damage and health, spawns a lot), Gremlins, (green-yellow, faster, but lower health and damage, spawns less often, but in groups of 3), Orcs (red, faster, stronger, and more health, spawns a lot less often), Trolls (mini-boss, dark green, slow, but lots of damage and health, spawns even less often), The Oni King (Boss, has a dash attack, Dark Red, fast, lots of damage, even more health. )
